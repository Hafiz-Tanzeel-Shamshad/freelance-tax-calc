How Freelance Taxes Work

Quick answers to the most common questions about self-employment taxes.

Self-employment tax is your Social Security and Medicare contribution. The rate is 15.3% on 92.35% of your net earnings ($168,600 cap for Social Security in 2026).
Q1: Apr 15 • Q2: Jun 15 • Q3: Sep 15 • Q4: Jan 15 (2027). Mark your calendar — missing a deadline can mean penalties.
Home office, internet, software, equipment, health insurance premiums, retirement contributions (Solo 401k, SEP IRA), vehicle expenses, business meals, travel, and education. Use our Deduction Finder to find every deduction you qualify for.
If you pay at least 100% of last year's tax (110% if AGI > $150,000) or 90% of this year's tax owed, you won't owe a penalty. Our calculators factor this in automatically.
