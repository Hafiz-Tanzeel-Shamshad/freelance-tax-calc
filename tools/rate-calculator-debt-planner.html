💰 Why Your Freelance Rate Needs to Be Higher

A $80,000 W-2 employee actually costs the employer ~$95,000+ when you add payroll taxes (7.65% employer FICA), benefits, 401k match, PTO, and training costs. As a freelancer, you absorb all of this — so your rate must cover the full employment cost, not just the salary.

📈 What Is Value-Based Pricing?

Instead of billing by time, you charge based on the value you deliver to the client. If your project saves them $50,000, charging $5,000 (10% of value) feels fair to both sides. The hourly rate becomes a side note — you're paid for results, not hours.
